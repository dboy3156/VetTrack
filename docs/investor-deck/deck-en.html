Veterinary today · hospital-grade operations

Why now

The cost of fragmented hospital systems

  • Patient & staff risk — critical information and actions scattered across tools; handoffs eat time in emergencies.
  • Operational leakage — billing, inventory, and utilization drift out of sync; leadership lacks a single truthful view.
  • Architecture debt — incumbent stacks are often weak at offline ward behavior, multi-site isolation, and real-time ops layers without heavy customization.

What we mean: Today many hospitals run a patchwork where each tool holds only a partial picture. The goal is a trusted operational system of record — one place where what happened in care, finance, and compliance lines up and can be defended afterward.

Differentiation

Not “another PIMS” — an orchestration layer with teeth

  • Vs. monolithic practice software: Legacy and cloud PIMS excel as systems of record for charting & billing, but teams still bolt on spreadsheets, WhatsApp, and whiteboards for floor tempo, asset location, Code Blue discipline, and leakage control.
  • Vs. single-point apps: Equipment-only or chat-only tools don’t join clinical action + stock + charges + audit into one tenant-safe stack.
  • VetTrack thesis: A strong integration + adapter base (per server/integrations/) lets the product act as a value layer on top of what hospitals already bought — sync where needed, own the workflows PIMS weren’t built for.

Market context (examples, not exhaustive): IDEXX (Cornerstone, Neo, ezyVet), Covetrus (Pulse, AVImark), Provet Cloud, Shepherd, Digitail, Vetspire, NaVetor, DaySmart Vet; specialty workflow vendors such as Instinct for ER/ICU-style operations. VetTrack competes for hospital orchestration, not necessarily for replacing the charting ledger on day one.

Flagship surface

Ward View — the live command surface

  • Purpose-built for the ward wall and charge nurse — prioritization, occupancy/equipment context, and “what changed” without digging through a classic PIMS queue.
  • Same security & offline engine as the rest of VetTrack — not a stale dashboard export.
  • Strategic value: Hospitals buy PIMS for records; they run the day on visibility — Ward View is where VetTrack wins daily stickiness.

Governance

Audit trail & operational proof

  • Immutable audit log of security- and finance-sensitive actions — equipment lifecycle, medications & tasks, Code Blue events, billing & inventory, integrations & admin (see server audit action types).
  • Searchable & attributable — who did what, when, in which tenant — critical for disputes, QA, and regulatory conversations.
  • Plus operational history — e.g. per-scan and room verification trails — so “what happened on the floor” isn’t hearsay.

Investor angle: Defensible operations are a moat in enterprise health — systems without deep audit lose enterprise deals.
