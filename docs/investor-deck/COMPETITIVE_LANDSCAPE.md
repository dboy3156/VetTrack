# Competitive landscape (high level)

**Purpose:** Investor-facing context. The market moves quickly—validate names and positioning before external use.

## Systems of record (practice / hospital management)

These are the dominant **PIMS / HMS** categories vendors pitch against. Many clinics treat one of these as the **legal and billing system of record**:

- **IDEXX** ecosystem: **Cornerstone** (on-prem legacy common in large practices), **Neo**, **ezyVet** (cloud, enterprise-oriented).
- **Covetrus:** **Pulse**, **AVImark** (long-tail on-prem installs).
- **Nordhealth:** **Provet Cloud** (multi-site / enterprise positioning).
- **Shepherd**, **Digitail**, **Vetspire**, **NaVetor** (Patterson), **DaySmart Vet** (ex-Vetter), **Hippo Manager** (portfolio changes via M&A—verify current ownership).

## Deep clinical workflow (especially ER / specialty)

- **Instinct** (and adjacent clinical workflow tools) often compete for **in-hospital treatment sheets, ICU-style workflows**, and specialty hospital operations—overlapping *where VetTrack’s Ward / emergency surfaces matter*, usually **alongside** a PIMS rather than replacing it entirely.

## How VetTrack is positioned (per product architecture docs)

- **Not only a PIMS replacement narrative:** VetTrack’s roadmap emphasizes **`server/integrations/` adapters**, encrypted tenant config, and **audit-heavy** operational modules—i.e. an **orchestration + ops intelligence layer** that can **integrate above** existing systems where hospitals keep their incumbent PIMS.
